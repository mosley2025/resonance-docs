Set up Resonance.

Resonance is a dark, editorial Shopify Online Store 2.0 theme built for product-led brands. This guide walks through everything from first upload to fine-tuning each section.

Need help at any point? Open the support portal and search the knowledge base or submit a ticket.

Welcome.

Out of the box, Resonance ships with a black background, white text, a sapphire accent, and a neon-cyan hover glow — but every color is yours to change. The theme is fully white-label: it works just as well for a cream-and-sage skincare brand as for a dark tech brand, and all demo copy is replaceable from the theme editor.

Everything in this guide happens in the Shopify admin under Online Store → Themes → Customize unless noted otherwise.

Quick start.

  1. Set your brand colors first. In the theme editor, open Theme Settings (the gear icon) → Branding Colors and set Background, Text, Accent, Glow, and Button. Almost every section inherits these automatically — set them once here before touching individual sections.
  2. Add your logo in the Header section settings (or leave it blank — the header shows your store name as styled text).
  3. Assign your menus. Header and footer each accept a menu from Online Store → Navigation. Until you assign one, tasteful fallback links are shown.
  4. Replace the demo imagery. Each image slot has a picker; bundled demo renders appear until you choose your own.
  5. Review each home page section top to bottom, replacing copy. Headlines are designed to end with an accent-colored period — the trailing period is added by the theme, so don't type one in the headline field.
Tip: not every color in a section follows the global palette. After changing Theme Settings, skim each section's own color panel and override anything that didn't pick up your new colors.

The color system.

Resonance uses a global-first model: every customizable section has its own color panel, and any color left unset falls back to the matching global in Theme Settings. Change the globals and the whole store restyles; override per section only where you want an exception.

ColorWhere it appears
BackgroundSection backgrounds. Adjacent sections with matching backgrounds blend into one continuous field — no divider seams.
TextBody copy. Meta text (dates, kickers, labels) uses the same color at reduced opacity.
AccentThin outlines and borders, the headline trailing period, the cart count badge, star ratings.
GlowHover halos only — the soft light that appears when you hover buttons and links. Never used as a text color.
ButtonOptional solid button fill. Left unset, buttons use a "tinted glass" look: a translucent accent fill with a thin accent outline that solidifies and glows on hover.

Most sections also offer: a Title color (falls back to Text), a border toggle + border color, and — where cards exist — a card tint color and opacity slider.

When you place text on a solid accent or button fill (badges, filter pills), the theme automatically flips the text to dark or light for contrast, based on the fill's lightness.

Resetting a color: to send a section color back to the global default, use the color picker's reset control (the ↺ / "reset to default" action) rather than deleting the hex value by hand — a hand-cleared field can leave a blank value behind.

Header & navigation.

Brand

Upload a logo (width adjustable 50–300px) or leave both logo and Brand text blank to show your store's name automatically.

Navigation

  • Menu: assign any navigation menu. Second-level items become dropdowns; enable Mega menu to render them as a full-width panel instead. A menu background opacity slider gives the dropdown/drawer a frosted-glass look.
  • Mobile: below 990px the nav collapses into a hamburger drawer that works even without JavaScript. Sub-menus become accordions.

Behavior

  • Sticky header with hide-on-scroll (default on): the header hides as you scroll down and reappears the moment you scroll up.
  • BUY NOW button: set its link, or leave blank to send shoppers to your all-products page (/collections/all). Optional hover text swap and a "static glow" checkbox (off by default) for a permanently glowing CTA.
  • Predictive search and the cart icon (accent badge shows the item count) complete the bar.

Announcement bar.

Resonance Announcement sits above the header — short messages (shipping thresholds, launches) with optional link, full color controls, and no seam border against the header.

Cart drawer.

With Cart type set to Drawer, clicking the cart icon opens a slide-out cart: quantity steppers, remove links, subtotal, and checkout — no page reload. Quick-add buttons on collection cards feed it directly. Switch to Page in Theme Settings → Cart if you prefer a full cart page.

Footer.

  • Menu: assign a footer menu; recommended links are About, Contact, FAQ, Shipping & Returns, Warranty, Privacy Policy, Terms of Service. (Create policies in Settings → Policies; their URLs are /policies/privacy-policy etc.) With no menu assigned, the footer builds a smart fallback from pages that exist.
  • Payment icons (default on) show your store's enabled payment methods.
  • Country and language selectors (defaults on) appear automatically once your store has 2+ markets or published languages — see platform notes.
  • Social icons come from Theme Settings → Social media.

Hero.

The full-bleed opener. Upload an ultra-wide image (2560×880 works best, with your product's focal point about two-thirds of the way across); on desktop the image frames the right side with a subtle mouse-driven tilt, and your headline sits left. On mobile everything centers over the image — an optional separate mobile image picker lets you supply a portrait crop.

  • Headline: rendered uppercase with an accent trailing period (added automatically).
  • CTA: label + link (blank link → all-products page). A background color + opacity slider adds a frosted fill; at 0 the button is outline-only.
  • Feature cards: up to several small image cards under the CTA, each with title, subtext, overlay tint + opacity, and an optional circular badge.
  • If your headline is hard to read over a bright image, darken the image, raise the card overlay, or set a Title color with more contrast.

Storytelling sections.

Ticker

An infinite marquee of short phrases separated by dots. A text opacity slider (default 30%) keeps it ambient. Edge fades match the section background automatically.

Feature Split (Resonance Precision)

A 50/50 block: headline + body + stacked feature items (label, description, icon, optional link) on one side, a large contained image on the other.

Spec Story

Chapter-style alternating image/text blocks used on the demo's Sound / Silence / Design pages. Choose image left or right per chapter; images crop uniformly to 4:3. Each chapter can carry an anchor ID for deep links.

Scroll Story

A pinned stage where 3–6 "beats" of image + copy crossfade as the visitor scrolls, with a progress rail. Falls back to simple stacked blocks when motion is reduced or JavaScript is off.

Specs grid

Icon + title + copy cards, 4-up on wide screens, wrapping neatly at every size. Card background and border colors are adjustable.

Image + Text

The general-purpose split (kicker, heading, rich text, optional button, image left/right). Powers the demo's About page.

Video

YouTube/Vimeo embed behind a cover image with play button — the video loads only on click, keeping pages fast.

Audio player. Signature

Resonance Audio Player lets shoppers hear your product — sound demos, sample tracks, ambience. Add track blocks, each with a title, caption, and audio source:

  • Audio URL: paste a direct link to an MP3 you've uploaded under Content → Files in your Shopify admin (upload the file there, then copy its link).
  • If both a URL and a bundled filename are set, the URL wins.

Keep files small (roughly 1 MB per 30–60s clip) for fast loading.

Image hotspots.

Resonance Image Hotspots places pulsing accent dots on an image; hovering or tapping a dot pops a callout card with a title and text. Each hotspot block has X/Y position sliders — drag the sliders while watching the preview to place dots precisely. Great for exploded views, ingredient callouts, or feature tours.

FAQ, compare & more.

FAQ

A native accordion. Blocks are either category headers or question/answer pairs (rich text answers, optional "open by default"). Left or center alignment.

Comparison table

2–3 columns with one highlightable column. Feature-row values of yes / no render as check / dash icons; anything else prints as text. Scrolls horizontally on small screens.

Testimonials

Glass quote cards with a 0–5 accent-star rating, quote, author, and role.

Newsletter

Email capture using Shopify's native customer form — subscribers are tagged newsletter and appear under Customers, ready for Shopify Email, Klaviyo, etc. All copy is editable, plus an ambient aura opacity slider.

Logo bar

An "As featured in" strip of press/partner logos.

Legal page

A quiet, readable layout for policies: kicker, title, optional "last updated" line, and a comfortable 720px column. Assign the legal page template to your policy pages.

Contact form

Standard Shopify contact form with the full color treatment and left/center alignment.

Product page.

Media gallery

Supports images, videos, YouTube/Vimeo, and interactive 3D models — each media item gets a thumbnail (with play/cube badges). Videos autoplay when their thumbnail is clicked. For 3D models, see the GLB guide; a 3D viewer background color setting is available.

Buy box

  • Variant color swatches (default on): options named Color/Colour render as circular chips; other options stay as pills.
  • Savings badge (default on): "Save N%" next to sale prices, recalculated per variant.
  • Sticky add-to-cart bar (default on): a slim fixed bar appears once the main button scrolls out of view.
  • Unit prices display automatically when set on a variant in the admin.
  • Trust badges: small check-marked reassurances (free shipping, warranty, returns) as blocks.
  • Collapsible panels: rich-text accordions after the description (shipping, warranty, in-the-box).
  • Promo strip: an offer banner with a copy-to-clipboard discount code and fine print. Create the actual discount under Discounts in the admin — the strip only displays the code.
  • "Complete the setup" upsell: pick up to 4 companion products.
  • Gift card recipient form appears automatically on gift card products: the buyer can send to a recipient's email with a message on a chosen date.

Recommendations

Resonance Recommendations ("You may also like") uses Shopify's recommendation engine and appears below the main product section. It needs a few days of store activity before suggestions get smart.

Collection page.

  • Filter drawer: a Filter pill (with active-count badge) opens a slide-in panel — collapsible groups, multi-select checkboxes with counts, price range inputs, Clear all / Apply. Active filters show as removable chips under the toolbar. Configure which filters exist under Search & Discovery (Shopify's free app).
  • Quick add (default on): a pill on each product card adds single-variant products straight to the cart; multi-variant products link to their page ("Choose options").
  • Sale badges appear only when a compare-at price is set; the badge text auto-flips dark/light for contrast.

Cart, search & blog.

  • Cart page: line items with order-notes field, continue-shopping link, and a styled empty state.
  • Search: predictive suggestions as you type, plus a full results page.
  • Blog: optional oversized "Latest" feature card for the newest post, posts-per-page control, show/hide toggles for image/date/author/excerpt, and card tint controls. Card images use each post's Featured image.
  • Article: share buttons, previous/next navigation cards, and a body font-size slider.
  • Customer accounts, order pages, login/register, and the password (coming-soon) page all follow your global colors.

Anchor links.

Most content sections have an Anchor ID setting. Give a section an ID (e.g. spatial-engine) and you can deep-link to it from any menu or button: /pages/sound#spatial-engine. This is how the demo's Technology menu jumps straight to specific chapters.

Tip: Shopify's link picker doesn't list /collections/all (the built-in all-products page) — type it into the URL field manually.

Platform notes.

Things that look like theme bugs but are Shopify platform behavior:

  • Price filter disappears in some currencies. Shopify hides the price-range filter when a shopper browses in a converted currency (e.g. EUR on a USD store). It returns in your store's base currency. Not a theme setting.
  • Language selector not showing? Languages must be both published (Settings → Languages) and assigned to a domain (Settings → Domains) before Shopify exposes them to themes. The country selector likewise needs 2+ active markets.
  • Editor preview ≠ real phone. The theme editor's mobile preview is a desktop browser in a narrow frame — hover effects and some scroll behavior differ. Always confirm mobile issues on a real device via Preview (share link) before reporting them.
  • Styles look stale in the editor? The editor sometimes caches section CSS — refresh the browser tab before assuming something is broken.
  • "View in your space" (AR) button on 3D models only appears on real mobile devices, not in the editor or desktop preview.
  • Meta description & social image are set in Online Store → Preferences, not in the theme.

3D models (GLB).

To show a product in 3D/AR, upload a .glb file as product media. For good results:

  • Real-world scale. AR places the model at its file size in meters — a pair of headphones should be about 0.2 m tall, not 1.2 m. Rescale before export (free tools like gltf.report can do this).
  • No WebP textures. iOS Quick Look renders WebP-textured models magenta. Keep textures JPG or PNG (watch out: some optimizers convert to WebP by default).
  • Keep it under 8 MB (Shopify's hard cap is 15 MB, but smaller loads much faster).

Support.

Search the knowledge base or submit a ticket at the Resonance Themes support portal — no account required. You can also email resonance.themehelp@gmail.com.

When reporting an issue, include: your theme version (1.0.0 shows under Online Store → Themes), the page URL, a screenshot, and whether it happens in the editor, the preview link, or the live store.
